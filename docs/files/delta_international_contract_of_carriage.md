# Delta Air Lines — Contract of Carriage: International

## RULE 1: General Provisions

Same core structure as the Domestic COC (contract = Ticket + Conditions of Carriage + Fare Rules). This document governs travel on any international itinerary; wholly-domestic-US travel is governed by the Domestic General Rules Tariff, and US–Canada travel by the Canadian General Rules Tariff. Rule 27 sets out country/region-specific rules for certain markets.

## RULE 3: Definitions (International-specific additions vs. Domestic COC)

- **Convention**: the Warsaw Convention (1929), the Montreal Convention (1999), or the Hague Protocol (1955) amendment, whichever applies.
- **International Carriage**: carriage where, per the contract, the place of departure and any place of landing are in more than one state (unless the Convention defines it otherwise).
- **Marketing Carrier** vs. **Operating Carrier**: the carrier selling under its code vs. the carrier actually operating the flight.
- **Special Drawing Right (SDR)**: IMF unit of currency, values published by the IMF and recalculated each banking day.
- **Delta Codeshare Partners**: carriers operating flights under contractual codeshare arrangements allowing Delta to sell tickets for their flights.

## RULE 6: Carriage of Persons with Disabilities

Identical framework to the Domestic COC (self-reliance declarations, Safety Assistant conditions, mobility aid acceptance, Service Animal policy, advance-notice services, boarding assistance).

## RULE 7: Refusal to Transport

Same grounds as Domestic COC, plus an international-specific ground: **D) Travel Across International Boundaries** — Delta may refuse transport when a passenger's travel documents are not in order or the transportation would be unlawful. Non-discrimination language additionally includes sexual orientation.

## RULE 12: Tickets

Same structure as Domestic COC. **Ticket Expiration** differs slightly: one year from issue date for commencement of travel; once travel commences, all travel must complete within one year of that commencement date (with reissue/exchange sub-rules). **Confirmation deadline**: payment/ticketing required at least **60 minutes** prior to scheduled departure (vs. 30 minutes domestic). International-only: **Additional Ticketing Fees** apply for tickets purchased directly from Delta Reservations in Europe.

## RULE 16: Travel Documents, Tourist Cards, and Travel Taxes and Expenses (international-only rule)

Passengers are solely responsible for obtaining travel documents and complying with all laws of countries flown from/into/through; Delta is not liable for advice given on this. A passenger denied entry or deported must pay the fare for return/onward transport as if originally ticketed to the revised destination; the fare paid to the point of refusal/deportation is not refunded unless required by the law of that country. A Transit Without Visa (TRWOV) service charge of USD 25 (or equivalent) applies where relevant.

## RULE 17: Baggage

Same general structure as Domestic Rule 17, with international-specific timing: refund of the checked-bag fee applies if the bag is not delivered within **15 hours** of arrival (international segments ≤12 hours flight time) or **30 hours** (segments >12 hours flight time), consistent with 14 CFR 260.5. **In-Bond Baggage**: baggage transported into the U.S. from a foreign point at passenger request/convenience is subject to a USD 50 processing fee per piece.

## RULE 18: Liability of Carriers; Codeshare Rules — Warsaw/Montreal Convention Regime

This is the core international liability framework, directly incorporating Convention limits (whichever Convention applies — Warsaw or Montreal):

- **Death/bodily injury**: Delta cannot exclude/limit liability for damages up to **151,880 SDR** per passenger. Above that threshold, Delta is not liable if it proves the damage was not due to its own/its agents' negligence, or was solely due to a third party's negligence.
- **Advance payments**: in death/injury cases, Delta must make an advance payment (without delay) to meet immediate economic needs; in death cases, the advance is not less than **16,000 SDR**, paid to a next-of-kin representative. Advance payment is not an admission of liability and is offset against any final settlement/judgment.
- **Delay damages**: Delta is not liable if it proves it and its agents took all reasonable measures to avoid the damage, or that such measures were impossible. Delay liability under the Montreal Convention is capped at **6,303 SDR** per passenger; airport/ATC/security personnel not under Delta's control are not deemed Delta's agents.
- **Baggage (checked and unchecked)**: liability capped at **1,519 SDR** per passenger for destruction, loss, damage, or delay, absent a special declared value with supplementary payment. This cap is waived for mobility aids (wheelchairs, walkers, scooters), which are compensated based on repair/replacement cost, with Delta arranging prompt repair (or a temporary replacement aid) at its own expense. Claims for unchecked baggage require proof of Delta's fault. An action for damages must be brought within **two years**; a complaint must reach Delta within **7 days** (baggage damage) or **21 days** (baggage delay) of the applicable date.
- **Limitation of liability (Rule 18(C))**: absent negligence, Delta is not liable for death/injury/delay/loss connected to carriage; not liable for damage from compliance with laws/government orders or beyond its control; not liable for one passenger's property damaging another's baggage (with an indemnity obligation on the responsible passenger); assumes no liability for pre-existing damage or ordinary wear; not liable for perishable spoilage or unsuitably-packed fragile items when Delta exercised ordinary care. Liability generally attaches only where Delta is the Marketing Carrier (except checked baggage, where the passenger also has a right of action against the first/last carrier). Delta disclaims liability for consequential/special damages in all cases.
- **Time limitations on claims**: damage-to-baggage complaints must be made "forthwith" and at latest within 7 days of receipt; delay/loss complaints within 21 days. Any right to damages is extinguished unless an action is brought within **two years** from arrival (or from when the aircraft ought to have arrived, or carriage stopped).
- **Codeshare-specific rules (Rule 18(H))**: for itineraries with at least one Delta Codeshare Partner segment, only standard-sized baggage is accepted (max 62 linear inches / 50 lbs, plus one carry-on ≤11 lbs/46 linear inches) across the *entire* itinerary, including Delta-operated segments; no unaccompanied minors accepted; no animals accepted except service/emotional-support animals and active-duty military family animals (per Operating Carrier policy); minimum check-in time is 2 hours (3h15m for Istanbul); extended tarmac delays follow the Operating Carrier's own contingency plan.

## RULE 19: Electronic Surveillance

Same as Domestic Rule 18.

## RULE 20: Flight Delays/Cancellations

Same structure as Domestic Rule 19, but the "significantly delayed/changed" threshold for international itineraries is **360 minutes** (vs. 180 minutes domestic) for both early-departure and late-arrival changes.

## RULE 21: Denied Boarding Compensation

Same structure and compensation amounts as Domestic Rule 20 (200% of fare, max $1,075, when Qualifying Alternative Transportation is arranged; 400%, max $2,150, when it is not), with an added **Codeshare Services** provision (Rule 21(H)): on a flight operated by a Delta Codeshare Partner, compensation is generally paid by the Operating Carrier at the time of denial, per this Rule (the Marketing Carrier's rules); if the Operating Carrier's payment falls short, the passenger may request the difference from Delta's Customer Care department.

## RULE 22: Rerouting

Same structure as Domestic Rule 21, with a nonrefundable-fare reissue procedure that nets the "change fee" against any refund/collection.

## RULE 23: Refunds

Same structure as Domestic Rule 22. International-specific nuance: taxes/fees on nonrefundable tickets are refunded only where required by law or collected in error (with proof of exemption), and a refund request must be received within one year of ticket issuance. Refunds are generally made in the currency and country of original purchase; involuntary refunds may, at passenger request and where legally permitted, be issued in USD or local currency at Delta's discretion.

## RULE 25: Limitation of Liability

International travel on Delta is expressly subject to the Warsaw Convention and Montreal Convention limitation-of-liability rules (cross-referencing Rule 18).

## RULE 26: Governing Law; Entire Agreement

Same as Domestic Rule 24 (U.S./Georgia law, entire-agreement clause, no punitive/consequential damages, English-language controlling text for translations).

## RULE 27: Geography and Country-Specific Provisions

Additional obligations apply to residents/citizens of China, Colombia, Dominican Republic, Israel, the European Union, Japan, Korea, Mexico, the Philippines, Ecuador, and Taipei (published at delta.com/us/en/legal/notices). Notably includes **Germany-specific alternative dispute resolution**: Delta participates in conciliation proceedings before the German arbitration body söp (Schlichtungsstelle für den öffentlichen Personenverkehr e.V.) for private (non-business) travel disputes involving cancellation, delay, denied boarding, involuntary downgrade, baggage delay/damage/destruction/loss, or breach of duties toward disabled/reduced-mobility passengers — available after the passenger has first raised the claim directly with Delta and remains unsatisfied with the outcome.
