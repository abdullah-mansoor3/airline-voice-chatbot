# Convention for the Unification of Certain Rules for International Carriage by Air (Montreal Convention, 1999) — Full Text

## Chapter I — General Provisions

**Article 1 — Scope of Application.** Applies to all international carriage of persons, baggage, or cargo by aircraft for reward (and gratuitous carriage by an air transport undertaking). "International carriage" means carriage where the place of departure and destination are in two different States Parties, or within one State Party with an agreed stopping place in another State (even a non-Party). Carriage by several successive carriers is deemed one undivided carriage if regarded as a single operation by the parties, even under separate contracts.

**Article 2 — Carriage Performed by State and Carriage of Postal Items.** Applies to carriage by the State or public bodies meeting Article 1 conditions. For postal items, the carrier is liable only to the relevant postal administration.

## Chapter II — Documentation and Duties Relating to Passengers, Baggage and Cargo

**Article 3 — Passengers and Baggage.** A document of carriage (or equivalent electronic record) must indicate places of departure/destination and any agreed stopping place in another State. A baggage identification tag must be delivered for each checked bag. The passenger must be given written notice that the Convention, where applicable, governs and may limit liability for death/injury, baggage destruction/loss/damage, and delay. Non-compliance does not affect the contract's validity or the applicability of Convention liability rules.

**Articles 4-16 — Cargo Documentation.** Govern air waybills/cargo receipts, their contents, evidentiary value, the consignor's right of disposition, cargo delivery, and customs/police formalities (largely outside passenger-facing scope but relevant to any cargo-arbitration extension of this system).

## Chapter III — Liability of the Carrier and Extent of Compensation for Damage

**Article 17 — Death and Injury of Passengers; Damage to Baggage.**
1. The carrier is liable for death/bodily injury only if the accident took place on board the aircraft or during embarking/disembarking.
2. The carrier is liable for destruction/loss/damage to **checked baggage** if the event occurred on board or while the baggage was in the carrier's charge — except where the damage resulted from the baggage's own inherent defect/quality/vice. For **unchecked baggage** (including personal items), the carrier is liable only if the damage resulted from its own fault or that of its servants/agents.
3. If checked baggage has not arrived within **21 days** after the date it ought to have arrived, the passenger may enforce the rights flowing from the contract of carriage (i.e., treat it as lost).

**Article 18 — Damage to Cargo.** Carrier liable for destruction/loss/damage during carriage by air, with exceptions for inherent cargo defect, defective third-party packing, act of war/armed conflict, or act of public authority related to entry/exit/transit.

**Article 19 — Delay.** The carrier is liable for damage from delay in carriage of passengers, baggage, or cargo, unless it proves that it and its servants/agents took all measures that could reasonably be required to avoid the damage, or that such measures were impossible.

**Article 20 — Exoneration.** If the carrier proves the claimant's (or the deceased/injured passenger's) own negligence or wrongful act caused or contributed to the damage, the carrier is wholly or partly exonerated to that extent. Applies to all liability provisions including Article 21(1).

**Article 21 — Compensation for Death or Injury.**
1. For damages up to **100,000 SDR** per passenger, the carrier **cannot** exclude or limit liability.
2. Above 100,000 SDR, the carrier is not liable if it proves the damage was not due to its own/its agents' negligence, or was solely due to a third party's negligence.

**Article 22 — Limits of Liability for Delay, Baggage, and Cargo.**
1. **Delay of passengers**: liability limited to **4,150 SDR** per passenger.
2. **Baggage** (destruction/loss/damage/delay): limited to **1,000 SDR** per passenger, unless the passenger made a special declaration of interest at check-in and paid a supplementary sum — in which case the carrier is liable up to the declared sum (unless it proves the sum exceeds the passenger's actual interest).
3. **Cargo**: limited to **17 SDR per kilogramme**, subject to the same special-declaration mechanism.
4. Weight calculations for partial cargo damage/loss consider only the affected package(s), unless value-to-other-packages effects apply.
5. Limits in paragraphs 1-2 (of this Article, i.e., not Article 21) do not apply if damage resulted from an act/omission of the carrier or its servants/agents done with intent to cause damage, or recklessly and with knowledge that damage would probably result (and, for servants/agents, only if acting within the scope of employment).
6. Courts may separately award costs/interest in addition to these limits, unless the carrier had already offered in writing an amount that would cover the eventual award (within six months of the incident or before the action began).

**Article 23 — Conversion of Monetary Units.** SDR figures are as defined by the IMF; national-currency conversion for judicial proceedings uses the SDR value at the date of judgment. Non-IMF-member States may instead fix liability at specified gold-based monetary-unit equivalents.

**Article 24 — Review of Limits.** The Depositary (ICAO) reviews the SDR limits every five years based on accumulated inflation (weighted average CPI of the SDR-basket currencies); a >10% inflation factor triggers an automatic revision (effective 6 months after notice, unless a majority of States Parties object); a >30% inflation factor allows any-time review at the request of one-third of States Parties. *(Per the current IATA guidance in the corpus, the most recent revision took effect 28 December 2024 — always verify current SDR figures against the latest ICAO revision rather than assuming these 1999 baseline figures are still current.)*

**Article 25 — Stipulation on Limits.** A carrier may voluntarily agree to higher liability limits or no limits at all.

**Article 26 — Invalidity of Contractual Provisions.** Any contract clause purporting to relieve the carrier of liability or set a lower limit than the Convention is null and void (without voiding the rest of the contract).

**Article 27 — Freedom to Contract.** The Convention does not prevent a carrier from refusing to contract, waiving available defenses, or imposing conditions that don't conflict with the Convention.

**Article 28 — Advance Payments.** In fatal/injurious accidents, the carrier must (if required by national law) make prompt advance payments to meet claimants' immediate economic needs; such payments are not an admission of liability and are offset against later damages.

**Article 29 — Basis of Claims.** Any damages action (however founded — Convention, contract, tort, or otherwise) can only be brought subject to the Convention's conditions and limits. Punitive, exemplary, or non-compensatory damages are not recoverable.

**Article 30 — Servants, Agents — Aggregation of Claims.** A carrier's servant/agent sued directly may invoke the carrier's own Convention defenses/limits if acting within scope of employment; aggregate recovery from carrier + servants/agents cannot exceed the applicable limit; this protection does not apply (for non-cargo claims) if the servant/agent acted with intent to cause damage or recklessly with knowledge that damage would probably result.

**Article 31 — Timely Notice of Complaints.** Baggage/cargo received without complaint is prima facie evidence of good condition/on-time delivery. Damage complaints must be made in writing within **7 days** (checked baggage) or **14 days** (cargo) of receipt; delay complaints within **21 days** of the baggage/cargo being placed at the claimant's disposal. Failure to complain within these windows bars any action except in case of carrier fraud.

**Article 32 — Death of Person Liable.** Actions proceed against the deceased liable person's legal estate representatives.

**Article 33 — Jurisdiction.** An action may be brought, at the plaintiff's option, before the court of: the carrier's domicile/principal place of business; the place of business through which the contract was made; the place of destination; OR (for death/injury claims only) a State Party where the passenger had their principal/permanent residence at the time of the accident, provided the carrier operates passenger services to/from that State (on its own or a partner's aircraft under commercial agreement) from carrier-leased/owned premises.

**Article 34 — Arbitration.** Cargo-carriage disputes may be arbitrated by written agreement; the arbitrator must apply Convention rules and the arbitration must occur within a Article-33 jurisdiction.

**Article 35 — Limitation of Actions.** The right to damages is extinguished if an action is not brought within **two years** of arrival at destination (or the date the aircraft ought to have arrived, or when carriage stopped).

**Article 36 — Successive Carriage.** Each successive carrier in a multi-carrier itinerary is subject to Convention rules for its own performed segment; the passenger/claimant may generally only sue the carrier that performed the segment during which the accident/delay occurred (absent an express whole-journey liability assumption by the first carrier); for baggage/cargo, the passenger/consignor may sue the first carrier and the consignee may sue the last carrier, with joint-and-several liability among the performing carriers.

**Article 37 — Right of Recourse against Third Parties.** Preserved regardless of Convention liability findings.

## Chapter IV — Combined Carriage

**Article 38.** For carriage combining air with another transport mode, the Convention applies only to the air segment.

## Chapter V — Carriage by a Person Other than the Contracting Carrier

**Articles 39-48 — Contracting Carrier / Actual Carrier.** Where a "contracting carrier" contracts with a passenger but an "actual carrier" performs all or part of the carriage under delegated authority, both carriers are subject to Convention rules (the contracting carrier for the whole contemplated carriage, the actual carrier only for the portion it performs). Acts/omissions of either are mutually attributed for liability purposes (capped at the actual carrier's own Convention limits unless it separately agrees to broader terms). Complaints/instructions to either carrier have equal effect (except Article 12 cargo-disposition instructions, which must go to the contracting carrier). Aggregate damages recoverable from both carriers combined cannot exceed the highest amount recoverable against either alone. An action may be brought against either or both carriers, in a court where an action could be brought against the contracting carrier (Article 33) or at the actual carrier's domicile/principal place of business.

## Chapter VI — Other Provisions

**Article 49 — Mandatory Application.** Any contract clause purporting to evade Convention rules (e.g., by choosing different governing law or altering jurisdiction rules) before damage occurs is null and void.

**Article 50 — Insurance.** States Parties must require their carriers to maintain adequate liability insurance under the Convention.

**Article 51 — Extraordinary Circumstances.** Documentation requirements (Articles 3-5, 7-8) do not apply to carriage performed in extraordinary circumstances outside a carrier's normal business.

**Article 52 — Definition of Days.** "Days" means calendar days, not working days.

## Chapter VII — Final Clauses

**Articles 53-57.** Cover signature/ratification/entry into force (effective on the 60th day after the 30th ratification), denunciation procedures (180-day notice), the Convention's precedence over prior Warsaw-system instruments among States common to both, provisions for States with multiple internal legal systems, and the narrow reservation allowed under Article 57 (State-operated non-commercial carriage; military-authority carriage on reserved-capacity aircraft) — no other reservations are permitted.

**Cross-reference note for the arbitration engine**: Every major carrier document in this corpus (Delta Domestic/International COC Rule 18/25, Southwest COC Section 8, PIA/Serene Air/AirBlue conditions) references these same SDR figures (100,000 SDR death/injury threshold, 4,150 SDR delay cap, 1,000 SDR baggage cap under the *original* 1999 text — though PIA's own published passenger-rights page cites a 4,150 SDR delay figure consistent with this text, and international air carrier practice generally cites the *currently revised* post-2024 SDR figures, which are higher than the 1999 baseline shown above). When arbitrating a claim, prefer the carrier's own currently-published Conditions of Carriage figure over this treaty's 1999 baseline figure, since the treaty figures are periodically revised upward by ICAO under Article 24.
