## I. Reserve Duty Period Definitions and Limitations

### A. Short-Call Reserve Definition

The FAA established definitions for reserve duty periods, specifically addressing short-call reserve classifications. Industry stakeholders, including NACA, Atlas, NAA, and Kalitta, raised concerns about the treatment of short-call reserve in the regulatory framework.

**Key Stakeholder Arguments:**
- NACA, Atlas, NAA, and Kalitta argued that limiting short call reserve to 14 hours is unwarranted for their operations
- Kalitta separately recommended that the reserve availability period should be 16 hours followed by 8 hours of duty under Kalitta's recommendation

### B. Reserve Availability Period vs. Flight Duty Period

The industry debate centered on how to distinguish between:
- **Reserve Availability Period (RAP)**: Time when flightcrew member is on reserve and potentially callable
- **Flight Duty Period (FDP)**: Time from flight duty commencement to final landing

**FAA Position:**
The FAA determined that:
- The full period of the WOCL (Window of Circadian Low point) should be considered rest rather than reserve availability
- The maximum reserve duty period includes both phone availability and/or FDP assignment
- Limiting reserve duty periods to assigned FDP is overly restrictive when FDP is a maximum of 13 hours

### C. Regulatory Provisions on Reserve Duty

**For Unaugmented Operations:**
If a flightcrew member is assigned FDP while on short call reserve, the FDP is measured from the time for reporting for assignment, limited to the Table B maximum FDP minus the full time spent in reserve during the Reserve Availability Period (RAP) up to the report time.

**For Scheduled vs. Unscheduled Operations:**
- Long-haul, non-scheduled operators may continue current operations under the proposed limitation
- Unaugmented operations have specific FDP measurement rules based on reserve assignment timing

### Table E Analysis

The FAA agrees that the proposed reserve provisions were overly complicated and made numerous changes to reduce complexity. The ARC (Aviation Rulemaking Committee) came to several conclusions during its discussion:

**Key Conclusions:**
- The FAA relies on the expertise presented in the ARC discussion
- The FAA does not support Kalitta's proposal to increase permissible reserve availability period to 16 hours
- Kalitta has not provided supporting rationale warranting modification of the collective opinion of the ARC

**FAA Decision:**
The adopted rule:
- Limits the short-call reserve availability period to 14 hours (the flightcrew member is not called to report to work)
- Addresses reserve duty period and unaugmented FDP provisions
- Provides that the total number of hours a flightcrew member may spend in a flight reserve duty period is limited

## III. Deadhead Transportation Duty

### A. Definition and FAA Proposal

In the NPRM (Notice of Proposed Rulemaking), the FAA proposed that all time spent in deadhead transportation be considered duty.

**Industry Position on Deadhead Transportation:**
The FAA further proposed that time spent in deadhead transportation would be considered part of an FDP if it occurred before a flight segment without an intervening rest period.

### B. Regulatory Treatment

**Circadian Rhythm Considerations:**
The FAA adopted provisions specifically addressing resynchronization of circadian rhythms. The rule:
- Ensures that flightcrew members have adequate sleep opportunity
- Particularly addresses those carriers conducting supplemental operations
- Uses 168 hours as the minimum trigger point for when rest must be provided for flightcrews returning home after completing FDPs that crossed multiple time zones

**Rest Period Requirements:**
Under Table F, flightcrew members would need to be provided a minimum of two nights' rest at home every 168 hours. This is considered an unrealistic constraint on the certificate holder's ability to rest and maintain a schedule.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8-9 | 10-12+ |
| 60-84h | 1* | 2* | 2* | 2* | 2* | 3 |
| 84-108h | 2* | 2* | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| 108-132h | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| 132-156h | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| 156+h | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |

**Table F Notes:**

**Note 1:** Eastward vs. Westward Transitions
- The values in Table F refer to eastward transitions (eastward outbound/westward homebound) only
- Asterisk (*) denotes that for westward transitions (westward outbound eastward homebound), one extra day is required to be added to the value depicted

**Note 2:** Recovery Rest Requirements
- When the elapsed time away from home base is less than 60 hours, one full physiological night's recovery rest should be provided on return to base
- Exception: When the returning flight duty period encroaches the WOCL, then an additional physiological nights rest will be added

### Table F Regulatory Analysis

**FAA Position on Table F:**
The FAA cannot support the inclusion of Table F. The rule could be accommodated given the rest period that was proposed without seriously constraining the certificate holder's ability to schedule operations. As discussed previously, the FAA:

- Decided to remove provisions that would permit a reduction in rest
- Adopts a provision that specifically addresses resynchronization of circadian rhythms
- That rest however must also be balanced with the certificate holder's flexibility to schedule operations, particularly those carriers conducting supplemental operations

**Restorative Rest Requirements:**
The FAA used 168 hours as the minimum trigger point for when this rest must be provided for flightcrews:
- Returning home after completing FDPs that crossed multiple time zones
- Under Table F, flightcrew members would need to be provided a minimum of two nights' rest at home every 168 hours
- This is considered an unrealistic constraint on the certificate holder's ability to schedule operations

**Cumulative Fatigue Considerations:**
While it is reasonable to anticipate that unforeseen circumstances may warrant a limited extension of FDP (particularly for situations that arise after takeoff), the flightcrew members at this point have already had the benefit of an eight hour rest opportunity. The FDPs limits implemented by this rule were derived under the premise that flightcrew members were reporting for duty with a full rest bank.

**Reduced Rest Provisions:**
- Permitting reduced rest undercutts that premise
- This applies provisions for extension of FDPs to the point that a rest period needs to be reduced
- Reduced rest should never be permitted since science supporting reduced rest assumes the one is starting from a full sleep bank, which is not always the case

**Consecutive Night Rest Reduction (168-hour period):**
- SWAPA commented that reduced rest should never be permitted since science supporting reduced rest assumes one is starting from a full sleep bank
- The FAA considers a one-hour reduction in rest only permissible in cases of unforeseen circumstances
- Subject to pilot in command (PIC) concurrence, it should never be permitted if associated with an extended FDP

## V. Regulatory Amendments and Summary

### FAA Final Decisions

The FAA regulatory provisions addressing reserve duty and unaugmented operations provide:

1. **Reserve and Unaugmented Operations:** Specific provisions that define reserve and unaugmented operation duty periods

2. **FDP Limitations:** Flightcrew members may not be assigned to duty periods exceeding maximum FDP values

3. **Rest Synchronization:** Circadian rhythm resynchronization requirements are incorporated, with criteria permitting a reduction in rest only:
   - In a 168 consecutive hour period, due to unforeseen circumstances
   - Subject to PIC concurrence
   - Never if associated with an extended FDP

4. **Certificate Holder Balance:** The regulations attempt to balance flightcrew member safety with certificate holder flexibility in scheduling operations

### Regulatory Framework Purpose

The rule ensures that:
- Flightcrew members have adequate rest opportunities
- Operations account for circadian rhythm considerations
- Reserve duty periods are appropriately limited
- Deadhead transportation duty is properly classified
- Recovery rest requirements are physiologically sound

## Appendix: Key Regulatory Concepts

### Window of Circadian Low Point (WOCL)
The period during which human circadian rhythm is at its lowest point, typically during early morning hours. The FAA considers the full period of the WOCL as requiring rest rather than reserve availability.

### Flight Duty Period (FDP)
Time from the moment a flightcrew member is required to report for an assignment until the moment the aircraft finally lands after that assignment.

### Reserve Availability Period (RAP)
Time during which a flightcrew member is on reserve and potentially callable to report for duty, but has not yet been assigned to a flight.

### Deadhead Transportation
Movement of a flightcrew member as a passenger from one location to another without serving as part of the operating crew.

### Augmented Operations
Flight operations with more than the minimum required flightcrew, allowing rotation of crew members for rest during flight.

### Unaugmented Operations
Flight operations with only the minimum required flightcrew, with no opportunity for crew rest rotation during flight.
