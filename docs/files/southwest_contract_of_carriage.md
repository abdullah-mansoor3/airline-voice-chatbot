# Southwest Airlines — Contract of Carriage (Passenger)

## Section 1: Introduction

Reservations/ticketing/transportation are subject to this Contract of Carriage in effect on the earliest of the date the ticket is reserved/purchased/issued, as amended through date of travel. Southwest may amend the Contract at any time in its sole discretion (changes must be written and approved by an authorized representative). If an amendment is made after ticketing but before travel, substantially affects the passenger's carriage terms, and the passenger does not agree to the amended terms, the passenger's sole recourse is a refund request (made before the travel date) per Section 4.c.(3). Southwest is not liable for consequential/incidental/punitive damages except where a limitation would violate applicable law.

**Key definitions**: Force Majeure Event includes acts of God, severe weather, pandemics/public health emergencies, government action, armed conflict/civil unrest, strikes/labor disputes, ATC/mechanical difficulties by other entities, or unforeseen situations. Significantly Delayed or Changed Flight means (due to a Southwest-imposed delay/change): departure ≥3 hours earlier (domestic) or ≥6 hours earlier (international) than scheduled; arrival ≥3 hours later (domestic) or ≥6 hours later (international); a different origin/destination airport; more connection points; downgraded class; or (for a disabled passenger) different connecting airports than originally itineraried. Flight Credit vs. Transferable Flight Credit™ distinguish Basic-fare vs. Choice-fare refund treatment; Transferable Flight Credits may be transferred once between Rapid Rewards members (with restrictions for corporate bookings).

## Section 2: Reservations

Reservation is confirmed by ticket issuance. **Cancellation**: a passenger/customer must cancel at least 10 minutes prior to scheduled departure to remain refund/credit-eligible; failure to do so triggers the No-Show Policy — Basic fare segments forfeit all funds/points; Choice fare segments convert to Transferable Flight Credit™; mixed-fare tickets follow each segment's own rule. **Prohibited booking practices** (hidden-city ticketing, no-intent-to-fly bookings, back-to-back round-trip combination, third-party bookings without consent, duplicate/impossible itineraries) allow Southwest to cancel unused ticket portions, refuse boarding/bag check, deny refunds, charge the "avoided" fare difference, and require repayment of any compensation provided. **Groups of 10+** must book through the Southwest Airlines Group Desk under separate group policies, which override the general Contract terms in case of conflict.

## Section 3: Fares

Fares apply per the ticketed date; a fare increase after ticketing does not affect an already-issued ticket. Post-purchase changes are subject to fares/rules in effect on the date of the change. Military and Government fares have specific eligibility/booking-channel restrictions. **Basic and Choice fares** are discounted, restricted, and nonrefundable.

## Section 4: Tickets

Tickets are non-transferable (with limited corporate-agreement exceptions for name changes ≥24 hours before departure). Non-compliance invalidates the ticket, allowing Southwest to cancel remaining itinerary, refuse boarding/bag check, and confiscate the ticket.

**Refunds (Section 4.c)**:
- **Refundable tickets**: fare (incl. taxes/fees) refundable for any reason upon surrender/cancellation, or convertible to Flight Credit/Transferable Flight Credit™ at customer election.
- **Form of refund**: credit card refunds typically ≤7 business days; cash refunds by check typically ≤20 calendar days; Rapid Rewards points redeposited with taxes/fees refunded per customer direction; gift-card purchases always convert to Transferable Flight Credit™; non-Southwest-channel purchases (travel agent, UATP) refunded via the ticket issuer.
- **Nonrefundable tickets**: Basic fare segments convert to Flight Credit on voluntary cancellation, UNLESS six months or more have elapsed since ticketing, in which case all funds are forfeited. Choice fare segments convert to Transferable Flight Credit™ regardless of elapsed time.
- Refund/credit requests must be made within one year of ticket issuance.

## Section 5: Check-in

Boarding passes obtainable via website/app/kiosk/skycap/ticket counter/gate podium. Failure to check in ≥30 minutes before departure, or failure to be present at the gate ≥10 minutes before departure ("Ten-Minute Rule"), may result in reservation cancellation without notice, at Southwest's sole discretion. Southwest may depart early once all checked-in passengers have boarded.

## Section 6: Acceptance of Passengers

**a. Refusal to Transport—General**: Southwest may refuse/remove passengers for safety reasons, Force Majeure Events, government regulation compliance, incompatible medical equipment needs (unapproved oxygen, incubators, aircraft-power-dependent devices, stretcher travel), or comfort/safety concerns (barefoot passengers over 5, inability to be seatbelted, any safety risk under 14 CFR Part 382, threatening behavior). Sole recourse is the refund value of the unused ticket portion; no special/incidental/consequential damages are owed.

**b. Refusal to Transport—Unruly/Disruptive Passenger**: extensive enumerated list of disruptive conduct (interference with crew/aircraft operation, refusing instructions, verbal/physical confrontation, refusing search/ID, fraud/misrepresentation, threats, boarding with infectious disease posing a direct threat, boarding with a weapon, intoxication, sexual harassment/abuse, lewd behavior, unauthorized in-flight solicitation, smoking, other riotous/threatening/belligerent behavior including harassment based on protected characteristics). Southwest may physically restrain, divert the aircraft, remove/refuse future carriage, and report to law enforcement. A passenger causing such conduct forfeits any damages claim (including ticket refund) and may be held liable to Southwest for repair/replacement costs, third-party damage/injury compensation, delay compensation to other passengers/crew, and diversion-related costs (landing/parking fees, fuel, passenger food/lodging).

**c. Carriage of Children**: infants <14 days old require a physician's written approval; infants 14 days–2 years may lap-travel with an accompanying passenger ≥12; unaccompanied children <5 not accepted; ages 5-11 require Unaccompanied Minor service (non-stop/no-aircraft-change flights only, not available for international itineraries) with a fee; drop-off/pickup requires ID verification and a signed release; persons <18 may be refused on overnight-connection itineraries unless accompanied by someone ≥18.

**d. Carriage of Passengers with Disabilities**: governed by the Air Carrier Access Act and 14 CFR Part 382; a Safety Assistant may be required in analogous circumstances to Delta's Rule 6(C); mobility/assistive devices carried free in addition to carry-on allowance, with liability capped at documented original purchase price per 14 CFR 382.131 plus reasonable consequential expenses; Trained Service Animals (dogs only, per 14 CFR 382.72-382.80) accompany the passenger free of charge subject to a completed DOT Service Animal Air Transportation Form and behavioral requirements; max two service animals per qualified individual.

**e-g. Pets, Law Enforcement/Search-and-Rescue Dogs, Military Working Dogs**: pets (cats/dogs, ≥8 weeks, in an approved carrier under the seat) accepted in-cabin for a fee, max 6 pet carriers per flight, not accepted on international itineraries or Hawaii-mainland routes; no pets in cargo hold; fully-trained law enforcement/search-and-rescue dogs and military working dog teams accepted free with proper documentation.

## Section 7: Baggage

**Liability cap**: **$4,700** per fare-paying passenger under 14 CFR § 254.4, with optional excess valuation coverage up to an additional **$1,250** (total max $5,950) for an added declared-value fee — excess valuation coverage explicitly excludes money, jewelry, photographic/video/computer/electronic equipment, software, china, fragile/perishable items, liquids, precious metals/gems, securities, business documents, art/antiques/collectibles, and similar high-value items. Southwest refunds excess/oversize/overweight charges if bags are delayed ≥12 hours (domestic) or ≥15 hours (international). **Claims**: loss/delay must be reported (with a Baggage report number obtained) within 4 hours of flight arrival or baggage receipt; damage claims within 24 hours; a completed Property Loss Claim form is required for lost-bag claims, submitted within 30 days. Southwest will not accept more than 20 checked bags per ticketed passenger. A "Limited Release of Liability" applies (at Southwest's discretion) to unsuitable baggage: voluntarily-separated items, fragile/unsuitably-packed items, previously-damaged items, over-packed items, perishables, soft-sided/unprotected cases, high-value items, and late-tendered bags (checked ≤45 minutes before departure) — Southwest disclaims liability for damage/loss/delay on all these categories.

## Section 8: International Travel

Governed by the Montreal Convention or Warsaw Convention, whichever applies, fully incorporated by reference. **Death/injury liability**: not excludable/limitable up to **151,880 SDR** per passenger; above that, Southwest is exonerated if it proves the damage was not due to its own/agents' negligence or was solely a third party's fault. **Advance payments**: minimum **16,000 SDR** in death cases, non-admission of liability, offset against final settlement. **Delay liability**: capped at **6,303 SDR** per passenger; Southwest exonerated if it proves all reasonable measures were taken. **Baggage liability**: capped at **1,519 SDR** per passenger absent a special declared value with supplementary payment; claims must be brought within **two years**, with complaints to Southwest within 7 days (damage) or 21 days (delay). International check-in minimum is 60 minutes (75 minutes for Aruba departures); gate arrival required ≥10 minutes before departure. Persons under 18 may not travel internationally unless accompanied by an adult ≥18 (special documentation may be required for single-parent or non-parent-accompanied minors).

Governs Southwest's handling of significantly delayed/changed/cancelled flights and denied-boarding oversale procedures (parallel in structure to Delta's Rules 19-20, adapted to Southwest's Significantly Delayed or Changed Flight definition above).

Governs carriage involving Interline Carriers under an interline agreement with Southwest on a single ticket.
