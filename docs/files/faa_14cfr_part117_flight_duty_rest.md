# 14 CFR Part 117 — Flight and Duty Limitations and Rest Requirements: Flightcrew Members

## § 117.1 Applicability

Applies to all flightcrew members and Part 121 certificate holders conducting passenger operations, including certain Part 91 operations directed by Part 121 certificate holders if any segment is a domestic/flag/supplemental passenger operation.

## § 117.3 Definitions (selected)

- **Acclimated**: a flightcrew member who has been in a theater 72+ hours, or has had ≥36 consecutive hours free from duty.
- **Augmented flightcrew**: more than the minimum required crew, allowing in-flight rest rotation.
- **Deadhead transportation**: passenger/non-operating transport of a flightcrew member as required by the certificate holder; counted as duty, not rest.
- **Duty**: any task performed as required by the certificate holder (flight duty, pre/post-flight duties, admin work, training, deadhead transport, aircraft positioning/loading/servicing).
- **Fatigue**: reduced mental/physical performance capability from lack of sleep or increased physical activity.
- **Flight duty period (FDP)**: begins when a crewmember is required to report with intent to conduct a flight/series of flights, ends when the aircraft is parked after the last flight with no further intended movement by that crewmember.
- **Physiological night's rest**: 10 hours of rest encompassing 0100-0700 at the crewmember's home base (or acclimated location).
- **Rest facility classes**: Class 1 (flat bunk, separate from flight deck/cabin, temperature/light/noise controlled); Class 2 (reclining cabin seat, curtain-separated); Class 3 (seat reclining ≥40° with leg/foot support).
- **Window of circadian low**: period of maximum sleepiness, 0200-0559 during a physiological night.

## § 117.5 Fitness for Duty

Each flightcrew member must report rested and prepared to perform assigned duties. No certificate holder may assign, and no crewmember may accept, an FDP if reporting too fatigued to safely perform duties; no certificate holder may permit a crewmember to continue an FDP if the crewmember self-reports as too fatigued to continue. Each crewmember must affirmatively state fitness for duty as part of dispatch/flight release.

## §§ 117.7-117.9 Fatigue Risk Management System (FRMS) and Training

A certificate holder may exceed Part 117 provisions only under an FAA-approved FRMS providing an equivalent level of safety, comprising a fatigue policy, education/training program, fatigue reporting system, fatigue monitoring, incident reporting, and performance evaluation. Annual fatigue education/awareness training is required for relevant employees (flightcrew, dispatchers, schedulers, operational control staff, and their management), updated every two years and submitted for FAA review.

## § 117.11 Flight Time Limitation

Maximum flight time: per **Table A** below for minimum-crew operations; **13 hours** for a 3-pilot crew; **17 hours** for a 4-pilot crew. Unforeseen post-takeoff operational circumstances beyond the certificate holder's control allow exceeding these limits only to the extent necessary to safely land at the next destination/alternate. Any exceedance must be reported to the FAA within 10 days with a description of circumstances.

## § 117.15 Flight Duty Period: Split Duty

For unaugmented operations only, a rest opportunity in a suitable accommodation does not count toward the FDP if: it occurs between 22:00-05:00 local; lasts ≥3 hours from arrival at the accommodation; is scheduled before the FDP begins; the actual rest provided is not less than scheduled; it is not provided until after the first flight segment; and the combined FDP + rest opportunity does not exceed 14 hours.

## § 117.17 Flight Duty Period: Augmented Flightcrew

Maximum FDP per **Table C** below (reduced by 30 minutes if not acclimated). Requires ≥2 consecutive hours of in-flight rest in the second half of the FDP for the landing pilot flying, and ≥90 consecutive minutes for the landing pilot monitoring. No more than 3 flight segments permitted under this section. At least one qualified flightcrew member must be at the controls at all times.

## § 117.19 Flight Duty Period Extensions

**Pre-takeoff unforeseen circumstances**: the pilot-in-command and certificate holder may jointly extend the Table B/C maximum FDP by up to 2 hours (extensions >30 minutes may occur only once before the next required rest period); may not cause exceedance of cumulative FDP limits (§117.23(c)); any extension >30 minutes must be reported to the FAA within 10 days with circumstances and (if within the certificate holder's control) corrective actions, implemented within 30 days.

**Post-takeoff unforeseen circumstances**: FDP may be extended as necessary to safely land at the next destination/alternate (may exceed cumulative limits in this case); the same 10-day reporting requirement applies.

## § 117.21 Reserve Status

Reserve is "long-call" by default unless designated airport/standby or short-call. Airport/standby reserve counts fully as FDP. Short-call reserve availability period is capped at **14 hours**; combined reserve-availability + FDP is capped at the Table B maximum + 4 hours (or 16 hours, whichever is less) for unaugmented ops, or the Table C maximum + 4 hours for augmented ops. Long-call reserve crewmembers must receive 12 hours' notice if the assignment would begin before and operate into their window of circadian low.

## § 117.23 Cumulative Limitations

Includes all flying on behalf of any certificate holder. **Flight time**: max 100 hours in any 672 consecutive hours; max 1,000 hours in any 365 consecutive calendar days. **Flight Duty Period**: max 60 FDP hours in any 168 consecutive hours; max 190 FDP hours in any 672 consecutive hours.

## § 117.25 Rest Period

No reserve/duty assignment during a required rest period. Before any reserve/FDP, a crewmember must have had ≥30 consecutive hours free from duty within the prior 168 hours. 36 consecutive rest hours in a new theater establishes acclimation. Crewmembers traveling >60° longitude and away from home base >168 consecutive hours must receive ≥56 consecutive rest hours (encompassing three physiological nights) upon return. Immediately before any reserve/FDP assignment, a crewmember must receive ≥10 consecutive rest hours providing a minimum of 8 uninterrupted hours of sleep opportunity; if this cannot be met, the crewmember must notify the certificate holder and cannot report until the full rest period is received. Deadhead transportation exceeding the Table B FDP limit triggers a rest period equal to the deadhead length (but not less than the standard 10-hour minimum).

## § 117.27 Consecutive Nighttime Operations

Up to 5 consecutive FDPs infringing the window of circadian low are allowed if the certificate holder provides a ≥2-hour rest opportunity in a suitable accommodation during each; otherwise the limit is 3 consecutive such FDPs.

## § 117.29 Emergency and Government-Sponsored Operations

Applies to U.S. Government contract operations and certain deviations under §119.57 where crew relief/rest cannot otherwise be provided. The pilot-in-command may extend FDP/flight-time/reserve-combination limits as necessary to reach the closest point where relief or required rest can be provided (but not beyond the §117.23(b)/(c) cumulative limits for Government-contract operations specifically). A rest period equal to the actual extended FDP (or 24 hours, whichever is less) must follow immediately. Exceedances must be reported to the FAA within 10 days with circumstances and corrective actions, implemented within 30 days.
