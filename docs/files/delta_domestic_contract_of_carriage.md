# Delta Air Lines — Contract of Carriage: U.S. (Domestic)

## RULE 1: General Provisions

**A. Contract of Carriage.** When you buy a ticket from or travel on Delta, you enter into a contract with Delta, and agree to be bound by its terms. The contract terms are set forth in: your Ticket; these Conditions of Carriage; and Delta's published fare rules and regulations ("Fare Rules"). This document is Delta's Domestic Conditions of Carriage, applying only to travel entirely within the United States of America. "Delta" refers to Delta Air Lines, the Delta Shuttle, and Delta Connection carriers. Delta may act as an agent for other carriers with interline agreements; for interline flights operated by other carriers, that carrier's conditions of carriage apply.

**B. Amendments to Conditions of Carriage.** Delta may amend these Conditions at any time except as provided by law. Travel is governed by rules in effect on the date of ticket purchase, except Delta may apply rules in effect on date of travel where reasonably necessary operationally and not materially negative to the passenger. No Delta employee or ticketing agent may modify any provision unless authorized in writing by a Delta corporate officer.

## RULE 2: Schedules and Operations

Delta will exercise reasonable efforts to transport passengers and baggage with reasonable dispatch, but published schedules, flight times, aircraft types, and seat assignments are not guaranteed and form no part of the contract. Delta may substitute carriers/aircraft, change schedules, delay/cancel flights, change seat assignments, and alter routings at its sole discretion. Delta's sole liability for such changes is set forth in Rule 22. Delta is not liable for missed connections or schedule/aircraft/seat changes.

## RULE 3: Definitions

Key terms include: Applicable Full Fare, Carrier, Co-Terminal, Days (calendar days including weekends/holidays, excluding date notice is sent), Dependent, DOT Hazardous Materials Regulations (49 CFR 171-177), Fare Component, Government Transport Request (GTR), Group, Immediate Family (spouse, domestic partner, children, step-children, grandchildren, parents, step-parents, grandparents, siblings, in-laws, aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews), Interlining, Military Agencies/Personnel, Person with a Disability (per 14 CFR 382.3), Personal Attendant, Qualifying Alternative Transportation (arrival within 2 hours of original planned arrival), Reroute, Round Trip, Routing, Safety Assistant, Self-reliant, Standby Passenger, Stopover (with a 4-hour connection exception), and U.S. Military Personnel/Armed Forces definitions.

## RULE 4: Personal Data

Passenger personal data given to Delta may be retained and transmitted to Delta's offices, other carriers, or service providers as required by law, per Delta's Privacy Policy.

## RULE 5: Inter-Airport Transportation

When a metro area has multiple airports and a passenger must connect between them, transportation between airports must be arranged and paid for by the passenger; baggage must be claimed and rechecked by the passenger.

## RULE 6: Carriage of Persons with Disabilities

A) Delta will not refuse transport solely based on disability, except as permitted/required by law. B) Delta accepts a passenger's self-declaration of self-reliance unless a Safety Assistant is deemed essential for safety. C) A Safety Assistant may be required when a passenger cannot comprehend safety instructions (mental disability), cannot physically assist their own evacuation (severe mobility impairment), or cannot communicate sufficiently to receive the safety briefing (severe combined hearing/vision impairment). D) No medical clearance is required except where Delta has reasonable doubt about safe completion of the flight. E) Seating accommodations, including with a Safety Assistant/Personal Attendant, will be made to the extent possible; disabled passengers are not barred from emergency exit rows except as required by law. F) Mobility aids (electric wheelchair, scooter, manual wheelchair, walker, cane, crutches, braces, communication devices, prostheses) are accepted free as priority checked baggage in addition to the regular allowance, and Delta assembles/disassembles them free of charge. G) Manual wheelchair use is permitted to the boarding gate and between terminal/aircraft where facilities permit. H) Service Animals (dogs only, trained to assist) are accepted for transport without charge and may remain at the passenger's seat (not occupying a seat); Delta may deny transport when reasonably necessary for comfort/safety. I) Upon request, Delta provides assistance with check-in, boarding area, boarding/deplaning, baggage, lavatory access, transfers between mobility aids and seat, limited meal assistance, periodic in-flight inquiries, and emergency briefings. J) Advance notice may be required for certain special services (electric wheelchair on small aircraft, hazmat packaging for batteries, groups of 10+ disabled passengers, onboard wheelchair, Service Animal in cabin on tickets <48hrs before departure, Service Animal on 8+ hour segments, dual vision/hearing impairment accommodation) — Delta will accommodate requests made ≥48 hours in advance to the extent possible, and make reasonable effort for shorter notice. K) Advance boarding is allowed where assistance with boarding/seating/carry-on stowage is requested. L) Delta will communicate announcements in a manner sufficient for disabled passengers to understand. M) Delta will periodically inquire about needs of non-independently-mobile wheelchair passengers awaiting boarding.

## RULE 7: Refusal to Transport

Delta may refuse to transport or remove any passenger for: A) government request/force majeure (weather, Acts of God, strikes, civil unrest, war); B) refusal to permit search for explosives/weapons/dangerous materials; C) refusal to produce identification; D) failure to comply with Delta's rules or the Contract of Carriage; E) passenger conduct/condition — Delta will not refuse disabled passengers (per 14 CFR 382.5/382.31) or refuse based on race/color/national origin/religion/sex/ancestry, but may refuse/remove passengers who are disorderly/abusive/violent/intoxicated, barefoot, interfering with crew, contagious, unable to be seatbelted, hazardous/risk-creating, seriously ill without physician permission, or whose conduct/attire/hygiene/odor creates unreasonable risk of offense. F) The sole recourse for a passenger refused/removed under this Rule is recovery of the refund value of the unused ticket portion, per Rule 22.

## RULE 8: Acceptance of Children

Children under 15 must be accompanied by a passenger ≥18 in the same cabin/flight (age verification documentation may be required). One infant under 2 travels free on an accompanying adult's lap (max 2 infants per adult); children ≥2 pay Applicable Full Fare. Unaccompanied travel: no child under 5 accepted unaccompanied; ages 5-7 non-stop only, no interline connections; ages 8-14 may connect on Delta/Delta Connection/KLM/Air France/Aeromexico only. Unaccompanied Minor Service is required for all passengers under 15 (Delta provides supervision from boarding to being met at destination but assumes no guardianship responsibility beyond that of an adult passenger); restrictions apply on last connecting flight of the evening and red-eye flights (with Hawaii/Alaska exceptions). Ages 15-17 may optionally request the service (fee applies). Delta may temporarily suspend unaccompanied minor travel due to weather/irregular operations.

## RULE 9: Specially Trained Service Dogs

Delta accepts, without charge, dogs trained to lead the blind, assist the deaf, assist physically/psychiatrically impaired passengers, or (accompanied by U.S. military/government handlers) for explosive detection/search-and-rescue. Service dogs stay in the cabin but do not occupy a seat.

## RULE 10: Smoke Free Service

Smoking and all smokeless tobacco products (including e-cigarettes/vapes) are prohibited on all flights.

## RULE 11: Passenger Medical Oxygen

Only FAA-approved Portable Oxygen Concentrators (POCs) are accepted for inflight medical oxygen; 48-hour notice required. Medical screening is provided free by Delta. Passengers using POCs must sit outside emergency exit/bulkhead rows.

## RULE 12: Tickets

Tickets are valid only for the ticketed routing and are non-transferable; presentation by someone other than the named passenger voids the ticket. If invalidated due to non-compliance, Delta may cancel remaining itinerary, confiscate the unused ticket, refuse boarding/baggage check, and/or assess the passenger the difference between fare paid and lowest applicable fare. **Ticket Expiration**: valid for one year from issue date; if exchanged, must be reissued and completed within one year of original issue; if space is unavailable due to flight cancellation, validity extends until space can be provided. **External Reissue Charge**: nonrefundable USD $50 fee for reissuing tickets originally issued by a non-Delta source (exceptions apply for same-day/irregular ops/schedule changes/SkyMiles upgrades/delta.com reissues/military-government fares). **Capacity Limitations**: Delta limits passengers per fare class/cabin at its discretion.

## RULE 13: Confirmation of Reservations

No reservation is valid until confirmed and entered in Delta's electronic reservation system. Payment and ticketing must occur at least 30 minutes prior to scheduled departure (or an earlier fare-rule deadline), or the reservation is cancelled without notice.

## RULE 14: Cancellation of Reservations

A) Delta will cancel reservations to comply with law/government regulation/emergency transportation requests, or due to weather/conditions beyond its control. B) TSA Secure Flight Program requires full name, date of birth, gender (redress number optional) at least 72 hours before departure, or Delta may cancel the reservation. C) Failure to appear for a flight without prior cancellation may result in cancellation of all remaining flights in the itinerary, with no remaining ticket value except where required by law. D) Reservations may be cancelled for failure to meet check-in/boarding deadlines; passengers must arrive with sufficient time for check-in, security, and government requirements.

## RULE 15: Voluntary Standby Travel

Tickets may not be used for voluntary standby on a different flight unless permitted by fare rules (not permitted for Basic Economy). Standby is subject to seat availability in the originally ticketed cabin; requests may be made up to 24 hours before original departure. A nonrefundable same-day standby fee may apply. Delta is not liable for denied boarding compensation for failure to accommodate standby requests. Eligibility/priority is at Delta's discretion (may consider ticket value, frequent flyer status, check-in time).

## RULE 16: Fares

A) Fares apply only between published points; tickets may not use a more distant published fare even if cheaper. B) **Erroneous Fares**: Delta may cancel a ticket purchased at an inadvertently erroneous fare and refund all amounts, or reissue at the correct fare, plus reimburse verifiable nonrefundable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in reliance on the erroneous fare. C) **Circumvention of Published Fares** is prohibited, including Back-to-Back Ticketing, Throwaway Ticketing, and Hidden City/Point Beyond Ticketing; violations allow Delta to cancel unused ticket value, refuse boarding/bag check, and/or charge the Applicable Full Fare. D) Combined fares are constructed to produce the lowest fare for the routing/class requested; Delta does not search alternative itineraries or combine unpublished/consolidator/negotiated/tour fares. E) Duplicate, fictitious, and impossible/illogical bookings are prohibited; Delta may cancel unticketed bookings, cancel-and-refund refundable tickets, or convert nonrefundable tickets to future-travel value as if voluntarily cancelled.

## RULE 17: Baggage

**A. Policies and Restrictions**: baggage policies/fees at delta.com/bags are incorporated by reference, restricting quantity/size/weight and governing hazardous goods and special items.

**B. Baggage Liability**:
1. Delta's liability for loss, damage, or delay of checked/carry-on baggage or personal property is limited to proven damage/loss, with actual value determined by documented original purchase price less depreciation. **Liability cap: $4,700 per fare-paying passenger.** If checked baggage is not delivered within 12 hours of arrival (domestic, consistent with 14 CFR 260.5, except circumstances in 260.5(f)/(g)), Delta refunds the checked baggage fee.
2. Delta is not liable for pre-existing damage or ordinary wear and tear.
3. **Special items**: no liability cap applies to wheelchairs/assistive devices, but Delta is not responsible for repair of damage existing at acceptance (noted on a release form). Delta is not liable for loss/damage to precious items (money, gift cards, antiques, documents, medications, electronics, jewelry, keys, manuscripts, paintings, securities, watches, irreplaceable items — receipts may be required), fragile articles, or perishable spoilage from delay.
4. No liability for loss/damage/delay resulting from government/airport security searches or confiscation.
5. **Claim time limits**: notice must be presented in person with the bag for inspection within 6 hours of arrival, except: damage to mobility devices, pilferage/non-apparent damage (24 hours), delayed bags (24 hours of return), and bags of Unaccompanied Minors/Persons with Disabilities (24 hours). Any action must commence within one year.
6. When multiple carriers are involved and responsibility cannot be determined, the $4,700 cap applies to all carriers; excluded items under another carrier's liability terms are excluded from Delta's liability too.

**C.** For codeshare flights (non-Delta-Connection), the marketing carrier's baggage rules on the first segment govern acceptance/fees, but Delta's baggage liability provisions still govern liability.

## RULE 18: Electronic Surveillance

Passengers and baggage are subject to electronic detector inspection with or without consent/knowledge.

## RULE 19: Flight Delays/Cancellations

**A.** For a cancellation, a ≥180-minute earlier departure, a ≥180-minute later arrival, a missed-connection-causing change, or a significantly delayed/changed flight: Delta will (at passenger's request, or if no alternative is offered/accepted) cancel the remaining ticket and refund the unused portion (including paid bag fees) in the original payment form per Rule 22. Otherwise, Delta transports the passenger on its next available flight in the originally purchased class, potentially via another carrier/ground transportation if acceptable to the passenger, with partial refund if downgraded. Delta is not liable for special/incidental/consequential damages from these changes.

**B.** Delta has no liability if the cancellation/delay/diversion was due to **force majeure** (weather/Acts of God; riots/civil unrest/war; strikes/labor disputes; government regulation; labor/fuel/facility shortages; other conditions beyond Delta's control or unforeseen facts). However, for delays >4 hours after scheduled departure NOT due to force majeure, Delta must provide: (a) hotel voucher for 10pm–6am delays (or travel voucher up to $100 if hotel unavailable) plus free ground transportation to the hotel; (b) ground transportation to destination for diversions within specified co-terminal city groups (SFO/OAK/SJC; LAX/LGB/ONT/SNA; DEN/COS; ORD/MDW; DFW/DAL; IAH/HOU; FLL/MIA/PBI; BWI/DCA/IAD; EWR/LGA/JFK; MCO/TPA/DAB/MLB/SRQ); (c) additional amenities as necessary for special-needs passengers (unaccompanied minors, disabled passengers).

**C.** Extended tarmac delays on codeshare flights follow the operating carrier's own tarmac delay contingency plan.

## RULE 20: Denied Boarding Compensation

**A.** Delta may sell more tickets than seats exist (overbooking), potentially resulting in an oversold flight.

**B.** Before denying boarding, Delta must first ask for volunteers to give up seats for compensation determined at Delta's discretion.

**C. Involuntary denied boarding priority order**: (1) Premium cabin/Diamond/Platinum/Gold Medallion/corporate-agreement ticket holders board ahead of Coach; (2) among Coach, passengers with boarding passes (obtained via online check-in within 24hrs, airport kiosk within 4hrs, or ticket counter) board ahead of those without; (3) among passengers without boarding passes: rebooked-due-to-irregular-ops passengers, then Silver Medallion, then SkyTeam Elite/Elite Plus, then no status — each group prioritized by class of service then check-in time; (4) special needs passengers (disabilities, unaccompanied minors, elderly/infirm, active-duty military) may be accommodated outside these priorities at Delta's discretion.

**D. Transportation for denied-boarding passengers**: Delta provides transport on its next available flight or, at its discretion, via another carrier, at no extra cost; if overnight stay is required, Delta provides hotel accommodation (or a travel voucher up to $100 if unavailable).

**E–G. Compensation for involuntary denied boarding**: No compensation is owed if the passenger failed to comply with ticketing/check-in requirements, if a smaller aircraft was substituted for safety/operational reasons, if an alternative cabin is offered at no extra charge (with fare-difference refund if downgraded), or if Delta arranges Qualifying Alternative Transportation (arrival within 1 hour of original planned arrival). **Compensation amounts**: 200% of the one-way fare to next stopover/destination (max **$1,075**) if Delta arranges Qualifying Alternative Transportation; **400%** of fare (max **$2,150**) if it cannot. Payment is made on the day/place of denial in cash or immediately negotiable check, or by mail/other means within 24 hours if the alternative transport departs before payment can be made.

## RULE 21: Rerouting

A passenger may change routing/destination/carrier/class/dates on an unused ticket (a one-way ticket cannot convert to round-trip/circle-trip/open-jaw after travel commences). Fare/charges for a requested reroute/destination change are those in effect on the date the change is entered. In-flight class upgrades require payment of the fare difference (no additional collection if the difference is negative or zero; subject to availability, no discounts apply). **Reissue procedures**: for nonrefundable fares, a lower-priced new ticket yields a nonrefundable travel voucher for the difference; a higher-priced new ticket requires collection of the difference. For refundable fares, a lower-priced new ticket refunds the difference to the original payment method; a higher-priced ticket requires collection of the difference. Unused tickets are cancelled and reissued at current fares (refundable value may carry over). Partially used tickets are repriced using whichever method (repricing flown segments vs. reissuing entirely) yields the lowest fare.

## RULE 22: Refunds

**A. Involuntary refunds** (due to cancellation, significantly delayed/changed flight, or refusal to transport not caused by passenger noncompliance): full fare refund if unused; if partially used, refund equals the fare for unused transportation from point of termination to destination/next stopover (at a fare breakpoint), or a mileage-prorated amount (within a fare component). No refund applies if Delta-provided alternate transportation is accepted.

**B. Voluntary refunds**: Most Delta tickets are **nonrefundable** — no refund of nonrefundable fare/taxes/fees except where legally required, though unused value (minus applicable charges) may go toward future travel. In the event of passenger death before travel, nonrefundable tickets are refunded to the estate upon request. **Fully refundable tickets** are refunded on request (unused portion including taxes/fees); amount = full fare if unused, or fare/taxes minus the used portion's value if partially used.

**C. Time limit**: refund requests must be made, and unused coupons surrendered, within one year of original ticket issue date.

**D. Form of refund**: credit card refunds typically within 7 business days; cash refunds by check typically within 20 days; UATP charges to the subscriber typically within 20 days; government transportation requests per applicable regulation; other payment forms back to original payment typically within 20 days.

**E. Overcharges**: no valid claim, and no Delta liability, for overcharge claims made more than 45 days after ticket issue date.

## RULE 23: Currency; Declined or Disputed Forms of Payment

Domestic fares/charges are stated in U.S. dollars. Passengers remain liable for the full ticket price notwithstanding any payment dispute, chargeback, or decline; Delta may collect owed amounts at any time, including after transportation is provided.

Delta is not liable for punitive, consequential, or special damages, nor for damage from compliance with laws/regulations/security directives or from passenger non-compliance or reliance on Delta's regulatory advice. Delta's failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver of its rights.
