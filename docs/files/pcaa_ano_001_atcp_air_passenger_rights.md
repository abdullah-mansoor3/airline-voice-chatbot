# AIR PASSENGER RIGHTS — AIR NAVIGATION ORDER (ANO-001-ATCP-2.0)

**Version:** 2.0
**Date of Implementation:** 30 September 2015
**Office of Prime Interest:** Air Transport & Economic Regulations Directorate, Pakistan Civil Aviation Authority

## A. Authority

A1. This ANO has been issued by the Director General CAA in pursuance of powers vested in him under Rule 4(3), 5, 180 and 360 and all other enabling provisions of Civil Aviation Rules 1994 (CARs 1994).

## B. Purpose

B1. The purpose of this ANO is to ensure that air passengers are properly compensated in case of Denied Boarding, Cancellations, Long Delays, Baggage Loss/Damage, Injury and Death, and Package Holidays.

## C. Scope

C1. Applicable to all airlines operating to and from Pakistani airports.

C2. The Director Air Transport & Economic Regulations is responsible for care, maintenance, review, update, revision, distribution and disposal of this ANO as per CAAO-001-MSXX (Document & Record Control Procedure).

## D. Description

D1–D10 (background/rationale): These regulations exist to safeguard the interests of traveling passengers and set minimum facilities that must be provided. The obligation rests with the carrier that performs or intends to perform a flight (own aircraft, dry/wet lease, or any other basis). These orders apply to scheduled and non-scheduled flights, including charters and package tours. Air carriers are required to reduce denied boarding by first calling for volunteers, to reduce cancellation trouble by giving reasonable advance notice and rerouting, and to meet the special needs of persons with reduced mobility. Passengers must be fully informed of their rights via ticketing documents and the airline website. Per the Montreal Convention, carrier obligations may be limited/excluded in extraordinary circumstances (political instability, weather, security risks, unexpected flight safety shortcomings, strikes) that could not have been avoided even with all reasonable measures taken.

### D11. Definitions

- **D11.1 Air Passenger**: person receiving air transportation from the air carrier for which remuneration is received by the carrier.
- **D11.2 Air Carrier/Airline**: a Commercial Air Operator operating with a valid Regular Public Transport (RPT) or Charter License under Rule 179 of CARs 1994.
- **D11.3 Operating Air Carrier**: performs or intends to perform a flight under a contract with a passenger or on behalf of another person having a contract with that passenger.
- **D11.4 Tour Operator**: an organization (other than an air carrier) providing package travel, package holiday and package tours.
- **D11.5 Reservation**: a ticket or other proof that the reservation has been accepted and registered by the air carrier or tour operator.
- **D11.6 Final Destination**: the destination on the ticket presented at check-in, or, for directly connecting flights, the destination of the last flight (alternative connecting flights are not counted if the original arrival time is respected).
- **D11.7 Person with Reduced Mobility**: any person whose mobility is reduced due to physical disability (sensory or locomotory), intellectual impairment, age, or any other cause of disability, whose situation needs special attention and adaptation of services.
- **D11.8 Denied Boarding**: refusal to carry passengers who have presented themselves for boarding, except where there are reasonable grounds (health, safety, security, inadequate travel documentation).
- **D11.9 Volunteer**: a person who has presented for boarding and responds positively to the carrier's call for passengers prepared to surrender their reservation in exchange for benefits.
- **D11.10 Cancellation**: non-operation of a flight previously planned and on which at least one place was reserved.

### D12. Requirements

D12.1 The requirements below are in addition to any rights of passengers under any other applicable law in force.

#### D12.2 Information About Flights and Reservations

- D12.2.1 When making enquiries or booking through an airline or its designated agent in Pakistan, each passenger must be given neutral and accurate information on flight details and reservation status.
- D12.2.2 Unless the passenger requires otherwise, the airline/agent must supply neutral information from a computerized reservation system, ranking journey options as: (1) non-stop flights, (2) flights with intermediate stops but no aircraft change, (3) connecting flights, (4) all applicable fares.
- D12.2.3–D12.2.4 The airline/agent must pass on, in the form of a computer print-out: the identity of the air carrier actually providing the service (if different from the ticket), aircraft changes during the journey, en-route stops, and airport transfers.
- D12.2.5 Full assistance (meals, beverages, communication facilities, and a hotel room for the night if necessary) must always be offered to stranded passengers, even if the delay or cancellation was caused by extraordinary circumstances or force majeure.

#### D12.3 Denied Boarding

- D12.3.1 When there are more passengers than seats, the airline must first call for volunteers to give up seats in exchange for agreed benefits, which must include a choice of either a ticket refund (with a free flight back to the initial point of departure, when relevant) or alternative transport to the final destination.
- D12.3.2 If the passenger is not a volunteer, the airline must pay compensation equivalent to **50% of the face value of the ticket**, excluding Government and Airport Taxes.
- D12.3.3 Compensation may be halved if the passenger is delayed not more than 4 hours.
- D12.3.4 The airline must also give the passenger: (a) a choice of ticket refund (with free return flight, when relevant) or alternative transport to final destination; and (b) meals and refreshments, hotel accommodation when necessary (including transfers), and communication facilities.
- D12.3.5 In case of denied boarding, the passenger is entitled to compensation and full assistance, plus alternate transport/re-routing at the earliest convenience subject to availability, or reimbursement of the full cost of unused ticket segments — provided the passenger checked in on time, including for non-scheduled and charter flights.

#### D13. Cancellation

- D13.1 Whenever a flight is cancelled, the operating airline must give the passenger: (1) a choice of ticket refund (with free return flight, when relevant) or alternative transport to final destination; and (2) meals and refreshments, hotel accommodation when necessary (including transfers), and communication facilities.
- D13.1.3 The airline may also have to compensate the passenger at the same level as for denied boarding, unless sufficient advance notice is given. Passengers must be informed about the alternate transport.
- D13.2 Refunds may be in cash, by bank transfer, or (with the passenger's signed agreement) travel vouchers, and must be paid as soon as practicable.
- D13.3 Passengers not receiving these facilities may complain to the operating airline.
- D13.4 **Financial compensation for delays**: the ticket amount will be refunded if the stranded passenger does not wish to continue the journey after a delay exceeding 5 hours; the passenger may cancel the flight on their own initiative and claim a refund of unused tickets. For cancellations, airlines may in some cases invoke force majeure and be exempted, but must inform passengers about the reason for cancellation; this written statement can subsequently be used in court proceedings to determine whether the carrier took all possible measures to avoid the situation.

#### D14. Long Delays

D14.1 The airline must provide immediate assistance if the passenger checked in on time (from a Pakistani airport, any flight including non-scheduled/charter; or for any flight to a Pakistani airport operated by a Pakistani operator from outside Pakistan), and the airline expects a delay of:

- **D14.1.1**: 2 hours or more, for flights of 1500 km or less;
- **D14.1.2**: 3 hours or more, for longer flights within Pakistan and for other flights between 1500–3500 km;
- **D14.1.3**: 4 hours or more, for flights over 3500 km outside Pakistan — the airline must serve meals and refreshments, hotel accommodation when necessary (including transfers), and communication facilities.
- **D14.1.4**: When the delay is 5 hours or more, the airline must also offer to refund the passenger's ticket (with a free flight back to the initial point of departure, when relevant).

#### D15. Later Claims

D15.1 When a Pakistani operator/airline is responsible for a flight delay anywhere in the world, the passenger may claim for resulting damage. If the airline disagrees, the passenger may seek redress in accordance with law. The claim may be made against the airline with which the passenger has a contract, or the airline actually operating the flight, if different.

#### D16. Baggage

- D16.1 The passenger may claim damages for destruction, damage, loss or delay of baggage on a flight by a Pakistani airline, anywhere in the world.
- D16.2 Damage to checked-in baggage must be reported to the airline representative and claimed within **7 days** of its return; delayed baggage must be claimed within **21 days** of its return. The claim may be made against the contracting airline or the airline actually operating the flight, if different.

#### D17. Injury and Death in Accidents

D17.1 The passenger may claim damages for injury or death from an accident on a flight of a Pakistani airline, anywhere in the world, and has a right to an advance payment for immediate economic needs. Redress follows the same contracting/operating-carrier logic as above.

#### D18. Package Holidays

D18.1 A passenger may claim damages from a tour operator that fails to provide booked services (including any flight in the package), regardless of destination. If the tour operator fails to provide a significant part of the package, it must assist the passenger with alternative arrangements, including travel, at no extra cost.

#### D19. Enforcement

- D19.1 A passenger affected by denied boarding, cancellation, or long delay and not compensated per this ANO may report the incident to the CAA Director Air Transport & Economic Regulations.
- D19.2 The Director will investigate and take necessary action to settle the issue.
- D19.3 Non-compliance may result in financial penalties against the Operator/Airline under Rule 334(3) of CARs 1994, as incorporated in the CAA Enforcement Manual.
- D19.4 Alternatively, the complainant may file a case in a Consumer Court against the airline for violating conditions of carriage.

#### D20. Enforcement Action / Financial Penalties

- D20.1 Enforcement actions in order of severity: financial penalties; suspension/cancellation of Air Operator Certificate; suspension/cancellation of RPT/Charter/Aerial Work Licence.
- D20.2 Progressive financial penalty schedule (per CAA Enforcement Manual):

| Occurrence | Standard Penalty | Serious Violation Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | Rs. 20,000 | Rs. 30,000 |
| 2nd | Rs. 40,000 | Rs. 60,000 |
| 3rd | Rs. 60,000 | Rs. 100,000* |

*Penalties exceeding Rs. 100,000 require CAA Board approval.

- D20.3 Recovery of penalties/fines/dues is enforceable as arrears of land revenue.
